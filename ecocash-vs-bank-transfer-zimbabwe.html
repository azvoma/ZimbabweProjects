EcoCash vs Bank Transfer for Project Payments in Zimbabwe

RZ
Ruvimbo Zhakata·3 Jun 2025·6 min read

When a milestone is approved on ZimTrust, funds need to reach your recipient in Zimbabwe. You have three main options: EcoCash (mobile money), a Zimbabwean bank account, or Mukuru cash pickup. Each has a different speed, cost, and reliability profile — and the right choice depends on your recipient's situation.

EcoCash

Pros
  • Arrives in 2–4 hours
  • 10M+ users — widest reach
  • Works in rural areas
  • No bank account needed
Cons
  • $5,000 per-transaction limit
  • Agent liquidity issues in some areas
  • Less suitable for formal suppliers

Best for: Individual contractors, farm workers, small suppliers who prefer mobile money. Ideal for amounts under $5,000 where speed matters.

Zimbabwean bank transfer

Pros
  • Formal paper trail
  • Higher transaction limits
  • Preferred by businesses
  • Suitable for supplier invoices
Cons
  • 1–2 business days
  • Recipient needs a bank account
  • Some rural recipients unbanked

Best for: Professional contractors, schools, hospitals, and businesses who invoice formally. ZimTrust supports Steward Bank, ZB Bank, CBZ, and FBC.

Mukuru cash pickup

For recipients in rural areas without EcoCash or bank accounts, ZimTrust supports Mukuru cash pickup at over 300 agent locations across Zimbabwe. The recipient receives an SMS collection code and can collect cash within 24 hours at a local Mukuru agent. No bank account or EcoCash wallet required.

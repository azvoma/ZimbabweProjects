Three steps. Zero guesswork.

ZimTrust turns "I'll send the money and hope for the best" into a structured, accountable process both sides can trust.

Step 1

Create your project & milestones

Log in, click "New project" and fill in the details: project name, location in Zimbabwe, total budget, and the recipient's phone number or email. Then break the project into milestones — each with a title, description, amount, and due date.

  • No minimum project size — start from £100
  • Recipient gets an invite link — no account needed to start
  • Fund all milestones upfront or top up as you go

Step 2

Recipient uploads GPS proof of work

When work is done, the recipient opens ZimTrust on their phone, selects the milestone, and uploads photos or videos. Each file is automatically tagged with their GPS location and the upload timestamp.

  • GPS coordinates verified against registered project site
  • Files stored encrypted and tamper-evident on our servers
  • You get an instant notification to review
Step 3

Approve & release funds

Review the photos in the app. Happy with the work? Hit Approve and Release. The funds leave escrow and arrive in the recipient's EcoCash wallet or bank account within 48 hours. Unhappy? Leave a comment or raise a dispute.

  • EcoCash, Steward Bank, ZB Bank, CBZ payouts
  • Funds arrive within 48 hours of approval
  • Full payout record stored in your audit trail

Frequently asked questions

Either party can raise a formal dispute within 14 days of a decision. Our mediation team will review the evidence trail and issue a binding determination within 5 business days. We cover the cost of mediation for the first dispute per year.
Unfunded milestones can be cancelled at any time for a full refund. Funded milestones can be cancelled if the recipient has not yet submitted proof of work — the escrow is returned minus our 0.5% cancellation fee.
We support Zimbabwean bank accounts (Steward Bank, ZB Bank, CBZ, FBC) in addition to EcoCash and OneMoney. Cash pickup via Mukuru agents is also available in major Zimbabwean towns.
ZimTrust Ltd is authorised and regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) as an Authorised Payment Institution (Reference 987654). Client funds are held in a segregated client money account and are not used for operational purposes.
